### 1) Detroit Zoo

![Detroit Zoo](assets/images/zoos/detroitzoo.jpg)

> *https://detroitzoo.org/* 

The Detroit Zoo in Royal Oak, Michigan is renowned for its disability access. 

Covering a substantial area of 125 acres, the zoo stands out as one of the best in Michigan, offering its visitors an incredible display of 245 species of animals such as giraffes, tigers, penguins, and zebras, among others. 

The zoo, with its unique combination of conservation, education, and family fun, represents a perfect spot for everyone, including families with older children. 

The zoo is notably user-friendly, with a mixture of paved, gravel, and woodchipped walkways, making it accessible regardless of some hilly and sloped areas.

Moreover, this large and diverse zoo takes 2-3 hours on average to see all of its attractions, making it an ideal destination for a full day of exploration. 

Despite some limitations in public transport access, there is ample parking available at a mere cost of $8 per car. 

Food stalls are conveniently located at the zoo, and if you prefer, you are welcome to bring your own food and drink. 

The ticket prices range between $14-23 for adults, $12-19 for children, with toddlers under 2 years enjoying free entry. 

Although pet dogs are not allowed, service animals can be accommodated. 

Therefore, the Detroit Zoo not only offers a memorable experience but also champions inclusivity, making it a popular choice for those seeking an accessible and enjoyable family day out.

Binder Park Zoo truly embodies its key philosophies of connection, inspiration, and conservation while offering a fulfilling day out for everyone.

Despite the lack of public transport access, the benefits it offers in terms of disability access make it one of the top zoos in Michigan.

Situated in the splendid Mackinac County of Michigan, the Garlyn Zoo Wildlife Park is an ideal destination for a family day out. 

The zoo is spread over a spacious 30 acres and hosts a plethora of fascinating creatures like tigers, sloths, bears, and lions which makes it an engaging afternoon for adults and children alike. 

With a ticket price of $15 for adults and $7.5 for children, it offers an economical retreat for a family of four who can enjoy an adventurous zoo trip for a total of $45. 

Plus, children under the age of 2 have the advantage of free entry. 

Despite the absence of public transportation, the zoo offers free parking facilities and allows families to bring their own food and beverages, enhancing the overall visitor experience.

What sets the Garlyn Zoo Wildlife Park apart from other zoos in the state of Michigan is its outstanding commitment to providing disability access. 

The entire zoo is navigable via mulch paths, making it accessible for individuals with mobility impairments - a crucial detail often overlooked by many hospitality establishments. 

This ensures that all visitors, regardless of their physical abilities, can partake in and enjoy the exploration of the zoo's myriad attractions, which on average, takes about 1.5 hours. 

However, it's essential to note that while pet dogs are not allowed, service animals are legally permitted within the zoo, supporting those who rely on these vital companions. 

Reach out to the zoo for more definite information. 

With all these provisions, Garlyn Zoo Wildlife Park holds an undeniable position as one of the best zoos in Michigan offering optimal disability access.

### 10) Wilderness Trails Zoo

Nestled in the heart of Saginaw, Michigan, Wilderness Trails Zoo is a delightful expedition into the animal kingdom catering to a diverse range of visitors, including those with disabilities. 

The natural trails winding through the expansive 56-acre zoo are suitable for wheelchairs, making it highly accessible for individuals with mobility limitations. 

Embracing over 200 exotic species within its folds including capybaras, bears, and lions, this zoological haven facilitates close encounters with these fascinating creatures, creating an immersive and riveting experience that leaves its guests deeply moved and inspired.

Wilderness Trails Zoo shines as a true gem among Michigan’s zoos not only for its rich biodiversity, but its strong commitment to inclusivity and accessibility. 

Visitors journeying through the zoo can expect to spend between 2-3 memorable hours on average exploring all of its unparalleled attractions. 

The zoo's pricing is also family-friendly with an average family of four only needing to spend around $60, making this unique experience more accessible to a larger crowd. 

Plus, children under the age of two are granted free entry! Despite not being accessible by public transport, the establishment provides ample free parking for visitors, and welcomingly allows guests to bring their own food and drink, a rare feature among similar attractions. 

The zoo follows a strict policy against holding animals, and while pets are not permitted, service animals are legally allowed entry, further underscoring the zoo’s endeavor to be accommodating to all. 

With such thoughtful features, it's no surprise that Wilderness Trails Zoo is considered one of Michigan's best disability-accessible zoos.
